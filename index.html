Anthony Rendon's Giants Baseball Cap

A nostalgic piece from Anthony Rendon's early baseball journey, this YMCA hat from 1995 captures a pivotal moment in the future major league player's life. Originating from Houston, Texas, the cap was donated by Bridget and Rene Rendon to the National Museum of American History. Measuring 7 inches by 4 inches by 9 inches, this artifact represents the grassroots of a professional baseball career, showcasing the personal history behind sporting achievements.

1953 Chicago White Sox Signed Baseball

A remarkable piece of baseball history, this autographed baseball represents the 1953 Chicago White Sox team, which finished the season with an impressive 89-65 record, placing 3rd in the American League. The ball features signatures from 20 notable players, including legends like Nelson Fox, Minnie Minoso, and Billy Pierce. Crafted by Zacko's in Pottsville, Pennsylvania, this leather, cork, and yarn baseball captures the spirit of a pivotal season in the team's history, preserving the signatures of key players who defined the era.

Colorado Rockies Owner's Baseball Cap

An unique artifact donated by Linda Alvarado, this baseball cap represents the leadership behind the Colorado Rockies. Meticulously preserved and documented by the National Museum of American History, the cap measures 7 inches by 5 inches by 11 inches. It symbolizes the critical role of ownership in professional baseball, highlighting the strategic vision and commitment that drives a major league team. As a gift from Linda Alvarado, it provides insight into the personal connection between team ownership and the sport's rich cultural heritage.
